Early Years

Controversial Birth Date

Beethoven (the third of his family) was born to Maria Magdalena Keverich and Johann van Beethoven in the city of Bonn in the Electorate of Cologne, a principality of the Holy Roman Empire. Although his exact date of birth is uncertain on documents, Beethoven was baptized on December 17, 1770. Based on law and custom, babies at that time were baptized within 24 hours of birth. Beethoven himself believed that he was born in 1772, and he stubbornly insisted on the incorrect date even when presented with official papers that proved beyond any reasonable doubt that 1770 was his true birth year. So, December 16 is his most likely birthdate.

Family Life

He had seven siblings, of which, only three survived through infancy, and only two survived into adulthood. Beethoven's mother was a slender, genteel, and deeply moralistic woman. His father (and first piano teacher) was a mediocre court singer better known for his alcoholism than any musical ability. However, Beethoven's grandfather, Kapellmeister Ludwig van Beethoven, was Bonn's most prosperous and eminent musician, and a source of endless pride for young Beethoven.

Abuse

Beethoven's father began teaching him music at a young age with extreme rigor and brutality in hopes that he would be seen as a child prodigy by Wolfgang Mozart. He practiced the violin and clavier with his father and took additional lessons from different organists around town.

On an almost daily basis, in order to learn and practice, Beethoven was beaten, locked in a room, and sleep deprived. This abuse affected him negatively for the rest of his life.

The Start of Recognition

Education

At age seven, Beethoven's father arranged his first public performance in March 1778. He was presented as a "little son of 6 years," (Mozart's age when he debuted for Empress Maria Theresia) when he was in fact seven years old. He played impressively, but his recital never received any press. Struggling with sums and spelling his whole life, he stated that "Music comes to me more readily than words." In 1781, at the age of 10, he dropped out of school to study music full time with Christian Gottlob Neefe. At the age of 12, he published his first composition, a set of piano variations on a theme by an obscure classical composer named Dressler.

Mozart

By 1784, Beethoven's father was no longer able to support his family due to his worsening alcoholism and voice decay. So, Beethoven formally requested an official appointment as Assistant Court Organist. Although he was young, his request was accepted, and he was put on the court payroll with a modest annual salary. To help him with his musical development, the court send him to Vienna in 1787 in hopes of being able to study with Mozart. After just a few weeks, he had to return to Bonn to be with his ailing mother. While there, he continued to improve his reputation as the city's most promising young court musician.

*Only speculation and inconclusive evidence exists that he ever met with Mozart, let alone studied with him.

Early Career

In 1792, Beethoven decided to move back to Vienna once again. Mozart had passed away a year earlier, and Joseph Haydn was now the unquestioned greatest composer alive. While in Vienna, he studied directly with Haydn and other eminent musicians and dedicated himself wholeheartedly to musical study. Finally, Beethoven made his long-awaited public debut in Vienna on March 29, 1795, playing his "first" piano concerto in C Major. Shortly after that, he decided to publish a series of three piano trios as his Opus 1, which brought him great acclaim and financial success. He continued to compose piece after piece over the years and mastered the Viennese forms developed by Mozart and Hadyn labeling him as a masterful composer. This is around the time he started to lose his hearing, most likely due contracting typhus in 1796.

Personal Life and Death

Life and Death after Recognition

Beethoven never married or had children probably due to his shyness and lack of good looks. He was involved in an ugly legal battle with his sister-in-law after his brother's death that lasted for seven years with him winning legal custody of his nephew. He lived a lonely and miserable life even though he created such beautiful music. He was paranoid, greedy, short tempered, and absent minded often feuding with his family, pupils, patrons, and friends. Beethoven died on March 26, 1827 (age 56) due to cirrhosis of the liver.
